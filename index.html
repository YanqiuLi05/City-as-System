STANHOPE STREET CURB STREAM

Hydrant: OFF Trash: 0
Rule: Hot = ON + high water level, Cold = OFF + low water level.
    A field study about the weather, public facility, trash and the people.

    Outside my house, there’s a fire hydrant that leaks almost every day. I found out it’s because some homeless people at the end of the street turn it on when the weather gets hot, and turn it off when it gets cold. When the hydrant is open, the water runs along the curb like a tiny river. Trash starts collecting there. Takis packages, milk tea cups, leaves, and sometimes even a dead mouse. No one cleans it when it’s wet, but as soon as the water dries, everything gets cleaned right away.

    I started to see this curb as its own little system in the city. The hydrant connects people, weather, trash, and the city’s cleaning schedule together. It’s meant for emergencies, but now it’s part of daily life, controlled by people who live here. The trash tells stories about what people nearby eat and drink. It feels like a “trash diary” that quietly records how humans, infrastructure, and nature interact every day in my neighborhood.

    This is a website is a "simulation" of the curb, built to generate the "trash diary".
